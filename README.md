
#💹**Analysis-on-E-Learning-Platform-purchase**

In this project, MySQL is used to design and analyze an E-Learning platform database containing Learners, Courses, and Purchases tables. SQL queries help analyze learner behavior, course performance, revenue, and spending patterns across regions.

----
#🎯**Key Insights**

• Revenue Drivers

Programming courses generate the highest revenue due to premium pricing
(₹2499–₹3999).
Business and Design categories contribute steady mid-level revenue.

• Top Performing Courses

Courses like AI Driven Data Analytics and Advanced Java Development show
strong demand.
High-priced courses are still selling well users value quality over price.

• Customer Behavior

Many learners purchase across multiple categories.
Indicates strong potential for cross-selling and bundled offers.

• Category Performance

Programming Highest revenue and demand
Marketing High volume, low revenue (low pricing)
Personal Development Lowest engagement

• Geographic Trends

Majority of users are from India.
Secondary markets include UAE, Canada, and Australia.

• Unused Assets

Some courses have zero purchases underperforming or poorly marketed.

---
#📍**Conclusion:**

Programming courses emerge as the primary source of revenue, while learners who enroll across multiple categories represent the strongest opportunity for future growth. At the same time, underperforming courses require immediate attention and improvement to enhance overall platform performance.
